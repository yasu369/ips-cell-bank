01 / Why the World Watches iPS Cells

iPS cells are not an accessory to future medicine. They are one of its foundations.

Regenerative Medicine and Precision Medicine are moving toward a future where the quality of cellular resources may matter as much as the quality of financial assets.

For families, founders, investors, and executives, private iPS cell banking is a way to preserve biological optionality before time, age, or illness changes the starting point.

02 / Kyoto University Origin

The innovation began in Japan.

Professor Shinya Yamanaka's work on cellular reprogramming at Kyoto University led to the 2012 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine. That discovery placed Japan at the center of the global iPS cell story.

03 / Why Autologous Cells

Your own cells are the most personal biological archive you can preserve.

Autologous cells carry the identity of the individual. Preserving them while healthy creates a reference point for future research, consultation, and medically appropriate options if they become relevant.

This is not a promise of treatment. It is a disciplined act of preparation: preserving a personal cellular resource under clear documentation, consent, and governance.
